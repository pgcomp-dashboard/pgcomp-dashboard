                                      8. 2018-2019. Stratification of patients suffering from myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome
                                        Descrição: Background: Myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/CFS) is a complex illness associated with disabling fatigue and a combination of symptoms including frequent episodes of pain and infections, and problems in concentrating and sleeping [1]. The decision of adopting a given treatment reveals has been challenging due to the wide spectrum of symptom profiles that can be observed in the ME/CFS population. Until now this identification has been challenging due to the unavailability of sufficiently large cohorts of ME/CFS patients. The creation of the UK ME/CFS biobank with data of more than 500 individuals including healthy controls and multiple sclerosis patients has opened the door to unique research opportunities including the stratification of ME/CFS patients. Objectives: The aim of this study is to stratify the ME/CFS patients into different clusters (or symptom subtypes). The respective objectives are the following: i) to distinguish ME/CFS patients from those suffering from multiple sclerosis (MS); ii) to identify sets of clinical symptoms that could differentiate different clusters of ME/CFS patients; iii) to identify the best (or exclusive) predictive symptoms for CFS and compare the results with those obtained from different statistical/computational methods; (iv) to compare the stability of patients stratification using baseline and follow-up data. Methods: ME/CFS biobank data related to clinical symptoms (baseline and follow-up) and background information will be analysed using a mixture of supervised and unsupervised multivariate techniques from Computer Sciences and Statistics. In particular, supervised machine learning techniques such as support vector machine will be used to distinguish ME/CFS patients from the MS ones. Unsupervised techniques such as cluster analysis, latent class analysis and partitional clustering will be used to identify subtypes of ME/CFS patients. Expected outcomes: The main research output will be a publication in an international peer-reviewed journal. The respective findings are expected to shed light into the aetiology of ME/CFS.
